History

    • Smoke inhalation

Considerations

  • Accidental/intentional exposure
  • Duration of exposure
  • Past medical history
  • Pregnancy

Signs & Symptoms

  • Altered mental status
  • Chest pain
  • Confusion
  • Dizziness
  • Dyspnea
  • Headache
  • Malaise, weakness, flu-like illness symptoms
  • Nausea
  • Seizure

Differential Diagnosis

  • Anaphylaxis
  • Cardiogenic syncope
  • Co-ingestant/exposure
  • Epilepsy
  • Hypo/hyperglycemia
  • Myocardial ischemia/infarction
  • Trauma/head injury
  • Viral infection

Basic Medical Care

  1. Ensure scene safety and a protective environment for all personnel and patients
    1. Consider additional precautions (distance and shielding) when radiological agents are involved
  2. Ensure fire department resources (Hazardous Materials Team) has been notified
  3. Attempt to identify exposure (bystander or worker information, incident location, environmental indicators, container description, placards or labels, shipping papers or Material Safety Data Sheets, patient symptoms)
  4. Don appropriate personal protective equipment
    1. Decision for type and level will be made by the scene Incident Command
  5. Immediately remove all patients from the exposure and determine the level of contamination present
    1. Determine the need for decontamination prior to full assessment and treatment
    2. Vapor material source: remove from source of contamination
    3. Liquid material source: remove contaminated equipment and clothing and perform gross and technical decontamination procedures
    4. Solid material source: remove material by physical measures of brushing away source, then gross and technical decontamination procedures as indicated
  6. Medical Initial Assessment Protocol or Pediatric Initial Assessment Protocol
  7. Airway: Adult or Airway: Pediatric Protocol
  8. Assess vital signs
  9. Provide supplemental oxygen per patient condition to maintain SpO2 = 94 - 97%
  10. Albuterol via hand-held or mask nebulizer as per patient condition {% if keys.ptYears > 0 %}
    Current Pt ({{ keys.ptYears }} years, {{ keys.ptKgs }}kgs):
    {% if keys.ptAdult %}
    1. Adult: 5 mg Nebulized
    2. Repeat 5 mg as indicated by patient's condition
    {% else %}
    1. Pediatric: 2.5 - 5 mg Nebulized
    {% endif %} {% else %}
    1. Adult: 5 mg
      • Repeat 5 mg as indicated by patient's condition
    2. Pediatric: 2.5 - 5 mg
    {% endif %}
  11. CPAP for persistent or severe respiratory distress per Respiratory: NIPPV Protocol
  12. Remove appropriate clothing to fully inspect the chest, abdomen, and extremities for any significant thermal injuries
  13. If available, utilize a Rad 57 to obtain carboxyhemoglobin level
    Initial SpCO level
    1. < 3%: Continue to monitor
    2. 3% to 12% and no symptoms: Oxygen via NRB mask
    3. > 3% and symptoms; or > 12%: Oxygen via NRB & transport
    Symptoms of CO toxicity
    1. Headache, vertigo, confusion, loss of consciousness
    2. Shortness of breath
    3. Nausea
  14. For eye exposure, copiously irrigate with sterile saline
  15. Provide copious water / saline irrigation to the any site of liquid chemical exposure burn
  16. Dry/powder chemical should be brushed-off
    1. Brushing away from potential inhalation of substance
  17. Apply sterile dressing to involved site(s)
  18. Any chemical information or copy of the data sheet (MSDS) should be brought to the hospital with the patient

Cyanide

Toxicity

  • Mild - no symptoms
  • Moderate - anxiety, nausea/vomiting, weakness, dizziness
  • Severe - syncope, loss of consciousness, seizures, apnea

Basic Medical Care

  1. Assess vital signs
  2. Provide supplemental oxygen
    1. High flow oxygenation is paramount for these patients
    2. Pulse oximetry measurements may be falsely elevated
  3. Assist ventilations as indicated by patient presentation
  4. Assess blood glucose level for any altered mental status

    Radiation Incident

    Basic Medical Care

    1. Ensure scene safety
    2. Start Triage Protocol
    3. Medical Initial Assessment
      4. or Trauma Initial Assessment Protocol as indicated
    4. Pediatric Initial Assessment
      5. or Pediatric Trauma Assessment Protocol as indicated
    5. Attempt to assess the type and duration of exposure
    6. Decontaminate with copious amounts of normal saline irrigation
    7. Additional care as per appropriate associated protocol


    Additional Info

    • Classes of radiation
      • Ionizing
        • Greater energy, most dangerous
        • Alpha particles, beta particles, gamma rays
      • Non-ionizing
        • Lower energy
        • Microwaves, radios, lasers, visible light
    • Mechanisms of protection from radiation sources
      • Limit time of exposure
      • Increase distance from source
      • Shield from source
