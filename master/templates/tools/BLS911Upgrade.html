ALS Upgrade Indications

Any of the following may indicate an ALS upgrade:

  • BGL < 60mg/dl & unable to take Oral Glucose; Diabetic Protocol
  • Traumatic Injury requiring Narcotic pain managment by a Paramedic
    • Pain rating > 7 and/or inability to take PO Acetaminophen
  • Symptoms consistent with potential Cardiac etiology:
    • Syncope, Chest Pain, Heart Problems
  • Necessity for IV Fluid Resuscitation:
    • Heart Problems - Hypotension
  • Necessity for IM medication administration:
    • Includes patients given IM Epinephrine by a BLS provider
  • Necessity for IV medication administration
  • Indication for a 12-Lead EKG
  • Clinical Impression:
    • ALS upgrade can be initiated if EMT clinical impression following assessment and/or treatment is that ALS evaluation or treatment is needed.
  • Necessity for any other ALS intervention

Any two of the following may indicate an ALS upgrade:

  • GCS < 12
    Exceptions:
    • GCS < 12 is baseline for this patient
    • Hypoglycemia; GCS corrected with Oral Glucose
    • Overdose; GCS corrected with Naloxone (Narcan®)
  • RR < 8
    Exceptions:
    • Overdose; RR corrected with Naloxone (Narcan®)
  • RR > 24
  • Systolic BP < 90mmHg
  • Systolic BP > 180mmHg
    Symptomatic Hypertension:
    • Chest Pain
    • Dyspnea
    • Altered Mental Status
    • Focal Neurological Deficits
  • HR < 60bpm
    Symptomatic Bradycardia
    • Syncope
    • Lightheaded/Dizzy
    • Shortness of Breath
    • Altered Mental Status
  • HR > 100bpm
    2 or more consecutive measurments
  • SpO2 < 90%
    Exceptions:
    • SpO2 is baseline for patient
    • Overdose; SpO2 corrected with Naloxone (Narcan®)
